Japanese Cuisine

Chef Training

This course teaches students the professional techniques and knowledge in 8 weeks, 12 weeks and 24 weeks(12 weeks lessons and 12 weeks internships). The curriculum covers all the Japanese Food culture. At the end of the course, the student will have gained knowledge in general Japanese culinary culture. It is an ideal course for those who aim to work professionally in the field of Japanese cuisine. The program includes following subjects:

Japanese Home Cooking
As in many other countries, the food served at restaurants is different from the everyday home-cooked meals. Learn how to make various “taste of mother” served at home on a daily basis, using all-natural ingredients and making soup stock from zero. You will also learn how to make lunch boxes for eating outside.

Japanese traditional foods Acquire the fundamental base of Japanese cuisine by learning traditional foods and ingredients which have been developed in deep relations with its diverse geographic and climatic character, as well as its style of living and the way the Japanese perceive life. Examples of recipes: Pickles, Umeboshi (Japanese plum pickles), Nimono (simmered dishes), Konnyaku, wild vegetables, different types of rice cakes, handmade seasonings, Agar jelly.
Wagashi (Japanese sweets) and Green Tea Wagashi (Japanese sweets) has its own world and philosophy reflecting Japanese culture. You will learn and acquire techniques for preparing a variation of sweets, with the context of seasons, occasions, and cultural background in which they are served.
